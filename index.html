Ram Mandir 2024

Rama was born to Kaushalya and Dasharatha in Ayodhya, the capital of the Kingdom of Kosala. His siblings included Lakshmana, Bharata, and Shatrughna. He married Sita. Though born in a royal family, Rama's life is described in the Hindu texts as one challenged by unexpected changes, such as an exile into impoverished and difficult circumstances, and challenges of ethical questions and moral dilemmas.[6] Of all his travails, the most notable is the kidnapping of Sita by demon-king Ravana, followed by the determined and epic efforts of Rama and Lakshmana to gain her freedom and destroy the evil Ravana against great odds.

On the auspicious day of approaching Paush Shukla Dwadashi, Vikram Samvat 2080, (dated January 22 Monday, 2024 CE), the new Vigrah (Deity) of the child form of Bhagwan Shri Ram will be installed in the sanctum sanctorum of the ground floor of the new temple being built on the birthplace of Shri Ram.

DETAILS OF SHRI RAM JANMBHOOMI TEMPLE

The temple is being built in traditional Naagar style. The length of the temple (East-West) is 380 feet, width 250 feet and height 161 feet. Three storey temple – height of every storey is 20 feet; total 392 pillars; 44 doors. Ground floor sanctum sanctorum – Deity of Bhagwan Shri Ram’s child form (Shri Ramlala – Presiding); First floor sanctum sanctorum – Shri Ram Darbar (Court of Ram). A total of five Mandapams (pavilions), viz., Nritya Mandap, Rang Mandap (pavilion with a centre stage), Guudh Mandap (assembly/esoteric pavilion), Prarthana/prayer pavilion, Kirtan Mandap. Carvings/Statues of gods and goddesses on the pillars and walls. Entry will be from the East (Singhadwar/Lions’ Gate), by climbing 32 steps (height 16.5 feet). Provision of ramps and lifts for the elderly and differently abled. Rectangle Parkotaa (ramparts/Praakaar) all around – length 732 meters, width 4.25 meters; four temples of Divinities Surya Dev, Shankar Bhagwan, Ganapati Dev, Goddess Bhagwati at four corners of the Parkota/ramparts; Bajrangbali Hanuman Ji on the southern flank/arm of the ramparts and Mata Annapurna on the northern flank. Pristine Sitakoop (well) dating from ancient Pauraanik Kaal/period near the temple. Other temples proposed on Shri Ram Janmbhoomi temple complex – Maharshi Valmiki, Maharshi Vasisth, Maharshi Vishwaamitra, Maharshi Agastya, Nishaadraaj Guha, Mata Shabari and Devi Ahilya. Renovation of the Shiva temple located on the Navratna Kuber Tila (mound) on the south-western part and installation of idol of the Ram devotee Pakshiraaj Jatayu.
